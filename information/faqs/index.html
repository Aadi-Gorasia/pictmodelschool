FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQs)

FAQS

Admission Process

The admission procedure will be updated on the website tentatively by the month of October-November. Kindly visit the ‘Admission Helpdesk’ page given on the website for precise dates.

Click on the Admission Enquiry Form given on the right-hand bottom of the school website. You will be directed to the ‘Admission Helpdesk’ Page. You are requested to read through the information and accept it by clicking on the “I understand and agree to proceed with the admissions process” button at the bottom of the page in order to proceed towards the Admission Enquiry form. After filling out the Admission Enquiry Form and successfully submitting the same, a system-generated token number will be sent to your registered email ID. Kindly save this token number for any further communication with the school. (Kindly check the spam, in case you have not received the automated mail in your inbox.)

Once the token number is generated, based on the availability of seats, the school may reach out to you within 15 working days. Should you not hear from us within this time frame, we may further reach out to you in the month of March 2025.

Refer to the Help Desk or contact us on our email.
Admission at PICT Model School is based on a first come first serve basis and enquiries/applications are accepted only through the website. For admission in the academic year 2025-26, please fill the Online Admission Enquiry Form. Kindly note that the Admission Enquiry Form closes upon receipt of the grade-wise expected number of enquiries.

In the eventuality of cancellation of admission by the parent before 31st March 2025, the 1st Installment fee and Caution deposit will be refunded. School will take a maximum of 15 days to process the refund. From 1st April 2025, in case of a cancellation, the refund will be on a pro rata basis. Should you have any other queries regarding the admission process, please feel free to mail us at admission@pictmodelschool.edu.in

Any Grade 10 learner from a recognized CBSE Board or an equivalent Secondary Board (ICSE, IB, IGCSE, State Board). Kindly read through 'Admission Helpdesk' page for more details on admission in Grade 11.

We offer Science, Commerce and Humanities streams for Grades 11 and 12. Subject combinations include Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, Biology, Computer Science, Accountancy, Business Studies, Economics, English, and optional subjects. Detailed information is available in our senior secondary prospectus.
For more information refer to the Admission Helpdesk

  • The prerequisite process of admission in Grade 11 includes student interaction which is conducted by a panel of educators at PICT Model School.
  • It includes attempting both verbal and written assessment which aims at gauging the interest, ability, and awareness of the learner on basic and general knowledge of the subjects opted.
  • Aptitude in Math is assessed irrespective of the stream chosen by the learner.
  • English Proficiency of the learner is expected to be at least CEFR B1 level.
  • Post the student interaction with the panel of educators, the school will recommend the most suitable stream along with the subject combination.

Should you be interested to avail the school transport facility, please submit the filled in transportation form to the transport in-charge on the very same day of interaction and requests received later will not be entertained. Transport is an annual service, paid in two installments. Once opted for, the transport facility has to be availed for the full academic year. The first instalment of transport fees has to be paid between 16th February and 7th March 2025. Please note that transport routes and fees will be shared before the window period of the payment opens. The second installment of transport fees has to be paid between 8th March and 10th June 2025. Kindly note that annual payment will be applicable for admissions after April 2025.

Cafeteria is a term-wise service. The first term cafeteria fees has to be paid between 16th February and 7th March, 2025 and the second term cafeteria fees has to be paid between 9th September and 22nd September 2025.

Other Information

The academic session starts by the end of March every year and continues till mid-March for the subsequent year.

Admission to PICT Model School is based upon vacancy in the class to which admission is sought. After the counselling session, should the parents wish to go in for registration, an interaction is arranged with the teacher and the child which is followed by a meeting with the Director Principal and both the parents to discuss the vision and mission of the institution. Should the parents believe in regulations that govern the institution, the school decides the admission.

School timings vary slightly for different sections (Pre-Primary, Primary, Secondary). Generally, the school operates from 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM. Specific timings for each grade are communicated at the beginning of the session.

The age criterion varies for each grade level as per government regulations and school policy. For example, for Nursery, a child should typically be 3+ years as of a specific cut-off date (e.g., June 1st). Please refer to our admissions page for detailed age eligibility for each grade.

Yes, sibling preference is given, subject to the applicant meeting all other admission criteria and availability of seats. Please mention sibling details in the admission form.

The school has CCTV surveillance, trained security personnel, controlled access points, and follows strict safety protocols. Staff are trained in child safety and emergency procedures. Visitor management systems are also in place.

Yes, we offer a wide range of extracurricular activities including sports, arts, music, dance, debate, coding, and various clubs to ensure holistic development.

Absolutely. We conduct regular professional development workshops, training sessions, and encourage our staff to attend seminars and courses to stay updated with the latest educational practices and subject knowledge.

Yes, we believe in a strong school-parent partnership. We encourage participation through PTMs, volunteer opportunities, workshops for parents, and involvement in school events and the PTA.

We strive to maintain a healthy student-teacher ratio to ensure personalized attention. The average class strength is typically around 25-30 students, varying slightly by grade level.

Yes, significant emphasis is placed on developing strong communication skills, including spoken English, through various activities, language labs, and classroom interactions.

Besides English (which is the medium of instruction), we offer Hindi and Marathi as second/third language options, depending on the grade level and curriculum.

Our campus includes facilities for various sports such as a playground for football and cricket, basketball courts, volleyball courts, and dedicated areas for indoor games like table tennis and chess.

In case of an emergency, parents must obtain a gate pass from the school office. The child will only be handed over to the parent/authorized guardian after verification.

Parents should submit a leave application through the school portal or a written note to the class teacher in advance. For medical leave exceeding a few days, a medical certificate may be required.

Primary modes of communication include the school ERP/portal, email, circulars, SMS alerts, and the school diary. Parents can also schedule appointments to meet teachers or school authorities.

Parent-Teacher Meetings (PTMs) are conducted regularly, at least once per term, and more frequently if needed, to discuss student progress and address any concerns.

We expect parents to be active partners in their child's education by supporting learning at home, ensuring regular attendance, communicating openly with the school, attending PTMs, and upholding the school's values and discipline.
